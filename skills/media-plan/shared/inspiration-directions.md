# Inspiration Directions

Use this file as the canonical inspiration library for media-plan ideation. These directions are not a mandatory checklist. They are optional strategy seeds that must still pass brief fit, research support, channel fit, and execution feasibility.

## Usage Rules

- Treat every direction as a hypothesis, not as a default answer.
- Prefer directions that match explicit brief signals plus recent public signals.
- Name directions to avoid when the brief or evidence says the fit is weak.
- Keep at least one wildcard direction outside this library when generating final idea options.
- Do not use low-trust or single-source hot takes as the only support for choosing a direction.

## Directions

### Time Anchors
- core_mechanism: Use a time window, festival, seasonal node, or recurring calendar ritual to create natural relevance and urgency.
- good_for: Categories with seasonal demand, gifting logic, travel peaks, or recurring user rituals.
- not_good_for: Always-on categories with no meaningful time trigger, or brands that cannot react inside the timing window.
- brief_signals: Campaign tied to a launch date, holiday, travel season, shopping window, or recurring annual moment.
- research_signals: Rising holiday discussions, platform seasonal content spikes, relevant travel or gifting chatter, or clear upcoming node timing.
- example_angles: Spring Festival co-branded launch, solar-term content series, Qixi scenario marketing, Labor Day or National Day travel tie-in.
- execution_moves: Start preheat about 30 days early, keep the brand tone stable, close quickly after the node, and differentiate instead of following the crowd.
- execution_risks: Generic holiday borrowing, weak brand linkage, slow launch timing, and post-holiday fatigue.

**Classic but watch out:**

| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Generic Spring Festival co-branding | 2018-2022 | Over-saturated | Every FMCG brand has done it; users are numb |
| Solar-term content series without brand link | 2019-2021 | Weak brand connection | Content gets shared, brand gets zero recall |
| Qixi "love story" scenario with no product truth | 2020-2023 | Product irrelevant | Pure emotional wrapping with no conversion path |

### Regional Hotspots And Local IP
- core_mechanism: Use a city's local culture, symbols, or regional emotional identity to create stronger relevance and shareability.
- good_for: Campaigns with explicit city targets, offline footprint, tourism scenes, regional product differences, or local KOL support.
- not_good_for: National campaigns with no local execution path or brands with no real local connection.
- brief_signals: Mention of a city, province, travel destination, local market expansion, offline event, or in-city conversion goal.
- research_signals: Local discussion spikes, city symbols, local creator activity, regional event calendars, or place-based user behavior differences.
- example_angles: City-limited co-branding, local dialect expression packs, fast response to a local hotspot, landmark route planning.
- execution_moves: Research the target city's symbols deeply, avoid stereotypes, work with local KOLs, and combine online with offline amplification.
- execution_risks: Cultural disrespect, shallow stereotypes, fake localization, and no on-the-ground activation path.

| City landmark check-in with no brand story | 2019-2022 | Shallow activation | Users post location, brand gets no association |
| Local dialect sticker pack as standalone content | 2020-2023 | No conversion path | Cute but doesn't drive to product or store |
| "City限定" packaging with no local supply chain | 2021-2023 | Fake localization | Claims city exclusivity but available everywhere |

### Cross-Category Collaboration
- core_mechanism: Create contrast and curiosity by pairing two worlds that do not normally appear together but still share a believable use case or audience overlap.
- good_for: Brands seeking earned media, co-brand packaging, limited editions, or new audience expansion.
- not_good_for: Products with no believable joint use case, no collaboration partner logic, or no operational support for co-launch.
- brief_signals: Need for brand refresh, collaboration openness, premiumization, youth reach, or cross-audience growth.
- research_signals: Successful co-brand cases, user overlap hints, discussion around unlikely pairings, or partner brand momentum.
- example_angles: FMCG x luxury, games x museum IP, tech x intangible heritage, food x beauty packaging.
- execution_moves: Check audience overlap without requiring full overlap, design a real use scene, and control release rhythm around peaks.
- execution_risks: Collaboration for attention only, no product truth, partner mismatch, and weak post-launch follow-through.

### Major Events And Competition Waves
- core_mechanism: Borrow attention from a major event, tournament, festival, summit, or industry gathering and anchor the brand into the live conversation.
- good_for: Brands with fast-response content ability, sponsorship assets, or clear thematic alignment with a known event window.
- not_good_for: Slow approval chains, no event relevance, or no ability to act while the event is live.
- brief_signals: Sports, music, gaming, industry conferences, sponsorship opportunities, or real-time engagement goals.
- research_signals: Event calendar certainty, audience attention concentration, rising related keywords, and platform conversation heat.
- example_angles: World Cup or Olympics borrowing, music festival presence, summit topic marketing, esports sponsorship and content.
- execution_moves: Plan early, define capture keywords, respond live during the event, and publish recap or follow-up after it ends.
- execution_risks: Arriving late, generic event wrapping, no real-time team, and attention evaporating before activation lands.

**Classic but watch out:**

| Slapping brand logo on generic "cheer for Team X" content | 2018-2022 | No differentiation | Every sponsor does this; brand is invisible |

| Event-themed product with no functional tie to the event | 2021-2023 | No product truth | Feels forced; doesn't drive trial |

### Culture Trends And Memes
- core_mechanism: Plug the brand into an active culture signal, meme, fandom, or internet emotion with enough speed and authenticity to feel native.
- good_for: Brands with fast content production, native social tone, and a high tolerance for short-cycle experiments.
- not_good_for: Slow governance environments, brands with low cultural permission, or campaigns that need long approval chains.
- brief_signals: Social buzz goals, youth culture relevance, entertainment tie-ins, fandom reach, or short-cycle content production.
- research_signals: Fast-moving meme spread, hit shows, viral creator formats, fandom interaction, or platform-native cultural codes.
- example_angles: Hit-drama scenario content, meme remixing, guochao design co-branding, virtual idol or digital human partnership.
- execution_moves: Move fast, keep an approval fast lane, use genuine cultural language, and exit quickly when the meme ages out.
- execution_risks: Forced meme usage, backlash from tone mismatch, IP misuse, and lagging behind the meme lifecycle.

| Generic "guochao" design with no cultural depth | 2020-2023 | Superficial patriotism | Visual cliché; no emotional resonance |
| Virtual idol partnership as pure spokesperson swap | 2021-2023 | No interactive depth | One announcement post, no sustained engagement |

### Technology Innovation And Builder Credibility
- core_mechanism: Use technology, experimentation, or expert proof to make the brand feel forward-looking and competent.
- good_for: Tech products, AI tools, B2B brands, innovation narratives, or launches that benefit from expert validation.
- not_good_for: Consumer campaigns where technology has no user value, or where the team cannot translate complexity into plain language.
- brief_signals: Innovation positioning, AI or new-tool messaging, thought leadership, white paper release, or builder community relevance.
- research_signals: New product launches, technical community attention, expert commentary, or category shifts caused by new tech.
- example_angles: Public-facing internal hackathon, AI tool experience campaign, scenario-based tech demo, research report or white paper release.
- execution_moves: Translate the tech into plain language, show real outcomes, use scenario stories instead of specs, and add expert or KOL validation.
- execution_risks: Feature dumping, fake innovation theater, no real demo, and weak audience translation.

**Classic but watch out:**

### Social Issues And Emotional Resonance
- core_mechanism: Use a social issue or shared public emotion to create deeper meaning and spread through recognition rather than novelty alone.
- good_for: Brands with real category relevance, long-term topic ownership, or credible user stories.
- not_good_for: Brands looking for quick moral theater, weak relevance, or one-off symbolic gestures.
- brief_signals: ESG, mental health, women, sustainability, social value, or audience emotion already central to the brief.
- research_signals: Ongoing public conversation, long-tail user sentiment, brand-rightness proof, and evidence of meaningful audience resonance.
- example_angles: Workplace mental-health content, women-focused thematic series, sustainability lifestyle campaign, anti-burnout or anti-involution storytelling.
- execution_moves: Anchor the topic to real brand relevance, let real user stories lead, and sustain the topic longer than a single event.
- execution_risks: Purpose washing, backlash from weak authenticity, moral overreach, and no sustained commitment.

**Classic but watch out:**

| One-off "support women" social post with no follow-up | 2019-2022 | Performative activism | Users demand sustained action, not symbolic gestures |

| ESG report as PDF with no user-facing story | 2021-2023 | Internal-facing only | Stakeholders read it; consumers never see it |

### User Co-Creation And Challenge Loops
- core_mechanism: Let users become the visible content makers so distribution grows through participation rather than only brand publishing.
- good_for: Social-first campaigns, creator participation, product trials, or categories with strong expression and remix potential.
- not_good_for: Products with high participation friction, unclear rewards, or teams without moderation and community operations.
- brief_signals: UGC goals, creator growth, trial conversion, social amplification, or community engagement.
- research_signals: Platform challenge norms, creator participation patterns, low-barrier formats, and incentive models that work in-category.
- example_angles: Douyin or Xiaohongshu challenge, product trial with real reviews, brand story submission, user work showcase or offline exhibition.
- execution_moves: Keep the threshold low, design a clear path to participate, make rewards transparent, and have the official account amplify early examples.
- execution_risks: Participation friction, unclear mechanics, reward disputes, and no operational follow-up.

**Classic but watch out:**
| Example | Period | Problem | Why not now |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Generic hashtag challenge with no creative framework | 2019-2022 | Low participation | Users don't know what to create; content is random |
| "Share your story" campaign with no amplification mechanism | 2020-2023 | No distribution | Users post but get zero engagement; incentive fails |
| Product trial with mandatory positive review requirement | 2021-2023 | Inauthentic UGC | Users feel pressured; reviews are obviously biased |
