# Pre-Brief Source Map

Use this file as the canonical source-priority map for pre-brief search. Default priority should favor China-market platform and social signals first, then use stronger editorial, consulting, or official sources to calibrate and verify what matters.

## Usage Rules

- Pre-brief search is for signal discovery, not final fact claims.
- Prefer recent sources, usually inside the last 30 to 90 days when the topic moves quickly.
- Platform and social sources are strong for spotting live signals, but they should not be the only support for major strategic claims.
- Bring in consulting, editorial, or official sources when the signal needs context, scale, or trust calibration.
- If signal quality is weak or contradictory, say so and keep the hint provisional.

## Source Buckets

### Platform And Social Signals
- priority: first
- best_for: fast-moving hotspots, creator formats, user emotion, live discussion, platform-native trends, meme detection
- trust_level: medium for discovery, low when unsupported by broader evidence
- use_in_pre_brief_search_when: the brief needs recent attention signals, social conversation, platform trend hints, or culture-liveness checks
- example_sources: Weibo hot topics, Xiaohongshu trending posts and keyword pages, Douyin trend signals, Bilibili hot discussions, Zhihu topic pages, WeChat ecosystem observation, platform official trend reports

### China Business And News Media
- priority: second
- best_for: brand moves, category news, campaign launches, event coverage, business context, industry developments
- trust_level: medium to high depending on source quality
- use_in_pre_brief_search_when: platform chatter needs editorial confirmation, or the brief needs a near-term business context layer
- example_sources: 36Kr, Huxiu, Jiemian, Yicai, The Paper, Caixin, LatePost when relevant

### Consulting And Industry Research
- priority: third
- best_for: trend framing, market shifts, audience behavior summaries, category benchmarks, medium-term planning context
- trust_level: high for structured context, lower for very recent micro-signals
- use_in_pre_brief_search_when: the brief needs strategic trend framing, size-of-change context, or user behavior interpretation beyond a news cycle
- example_sources: QuestMobile, iResearch, NielsenIQ, McKinsey, Bain, BCG, Deloitte, PwC

### Official And Public Data
- priority: fourth
- best_for: baseline validation, macro context, sector statistics, regional policy signals, tourism or consumption data
- trust_level: high
- use_in_pre_brief_search_when: strategic claims need a stable baseline, regional planning matters, or public data can prevent over-reading noisy signals
- example_sources: National Bureau of Statistics, Ministry of Culture and Tourism, local commerce bureaus, local tourism bureaus, industry associations

## Selection Heuristics

- If the brief is social-first, youth-facing, or content-led, start with platform and social signals.
- If the brief mentions city, region, travel, or offline activation, add local official and local media sources early.
- If the brief is B2B, innovation-led, or category-education heavy, elevate consulting and research sources sooner.
- If a platform signal is strong but source trust is weak, treat it as a hint to verify rather than as a final conclusion.
- If multiple buckets disagree, document the tension instead of forcing a clean story.
